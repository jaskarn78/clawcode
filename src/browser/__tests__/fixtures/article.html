How to Train Your Bot

By Clawdy, April 18 2026

Training a browser-automation bot is equal parts patience and cynicism. You start by teaching it to navigate a URL and read the title, which is exactly the kind of task that looks trivial until you meet a single-page application that redraws its DOM seven times during the initial load.

The second lesson is that selectors age badly. A CSS class that worked on Monday will disappear by Friday because a frontend engineer discovered a new Tailwind utility and refactored the entire design system. Prefer role-based selectors, data-testid attributes, and text content — they survive redesigns better.

The third and final lesson is that every browser automation story ends with someone timing out on a network-idle wait. Default to the load event and graduate to domcontentloaded when you need speed; never rely on networkidle for SPAs that poll every few seconds for live updates.

With those three rules internalized, the rest of the curriculum is implementation detail: error handling, retry budgets, and knowing when the answer is to take a screenshot and hand it to a human reviewer.
